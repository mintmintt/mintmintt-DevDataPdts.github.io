Your BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is defined as the minimum number of calories your body burns whether at rest, working out or lying down. This means that everyone has a set number of calories that they need in order to stay alive. These calories are the absolute minimum amount of energy your body burns, and includes all involuntary (independent of your own will) activities that your body does to stay alive such as digestion, respiration, circulation, removing waste products and regulating body temperature. ======================================================== The following formula was used to determine your basal metabolic rate (BMR). W = weight in kg H = height in cm A = age in years BMR = 66.47 + (13.75 x W) + (5.0 x H) - (6.75 x A) The result was indicated on three ranges of Below Average: < 1000 Average: < 1200 Above Average: < 1800 For example, if you are 25 years old, and your weight is 65 kg with a height of 180 cm, your BMR is 1691.47. ======================================================== Once you know your BMR, you can easily calculate many other things, such as your total caloric needs for your individual daily activity type. Your daily activity type includes: Sedentary Lightly Active Moderately Active Very Active Extremely Active With BMR, you will have the necessary information regarding how much calories you need to consume if you want to lose weight.
